Background
The Arab Spring was series of protests across North Africa and the Middle East during 2011 and 2012 (see map here). These were popular uprisings by everyday citizens against their authoritarian, or non-democratic, governments (see timeline here). Their grievances included economic demands for less unemployment and inflation and political demands for greater freedom and inclusion. In some cases, these protests succeeded and revolutions occurred replacing autocrats. This happened in Tunisia early in the Arab Spring. In other instances, the protests failed and the dictators remained. Syria collapsed into civil war which continues today. What happened across the region varied and our particular focus will be Egypt. If you want to learn more about the Arab Spring go here and here. Note: Much has happened since 2012 but our focus will be on these two years in this one country.
